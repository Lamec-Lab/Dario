\begin{resumo}[Abstract]

% >>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>
\begin{otherlanguage*}{english}


In railway engineering, the control of vibrations generated by train traffic is essential to ensure the structural integrity of surrounding constructions and to minimize discomfort to the population. In this context, metamaterials and periodic structures have been studied for their ability to filter waves through the formation of bandgaps, frequency ranges where vibration transmission is significantly reduced.
In this work, a numerical analysis of wave propagation was performed in two different configurations of periodic structures: a metamaterial beam coupled to periodically distributed resonators and a railway track with equally spaced supports. For this, the Wave and Finite Element Method were used, aiming to identify the bandgap occurrence ranges and evaluate the effect of different geometric and material parameters on the vibrational response.
In the metamaterial beam, the influence of the number of resonators, the lattice constant and the material composition of the unit cell were analyzed. It was found that increasing the number of resonators generates new bandgaps by local resonance, while increasing the spacing reduces the width of these bands and shifts the Bragg bandgaps to lower frequencies. The use of different materials in the unit cell proved to be effective in adjusting the position and width of the attenuation bands.
In the periodic railway track, the effect of the vertical and lateral stiffness of the support pads, as well as the spacing between sleepers, was studied. The results demonstrated that increasing the stiffness widens the bandgap occurrence ranges. The variation in the spacing allowed the bandgap frequency bands to be shifted, also changing their width. It is concluded that the appropriate design of periodic structures allows for significantly improving vibration isolation, being a promising strategy for applications in mechanical and railway systems.

\end{otherlanguage*}%{english}
\end{resumo}
